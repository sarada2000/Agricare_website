1. Prevention

Cultural Control

The foundation of healthy farming. Stop the disease before it starts.

  • Crop Rotation: Rotate with legumes to break fungal life cycles.
  • Fallow Period: Leave field dry for 2-3 weeks to kill spores.
  • Clean Seeds: Use Department of Agriculture certified seeds only.
  • Spacing: Maintain 20cm gaps to reduce humidity.

2. Nature's Army

Biological Control

Using living organisms and natural enemies to control pest populations.

  • Predators: Protect Dragonflies and Spiders; they eat Planthoppers.
  • Trichogramma: Release these parasitic wasps to destroy stem borer eggs.
  • Ducks: Release ducks into the field to eat snails and weeds.

3. Last Resort

Chemical Control

Use synthetic pesticides only when absolutely necessary.

  • Thresholds: Only spray if damage exceeds 5% of the crop.
  • Fungicides: Use Tebuconazole or Azoxystrobin (Apply early morning).
  • Safety: Always wear masks, gloves, and boots.
  • Pre-Harvest: Stop spraying 14 days before harvest.
