Sheath Blight

A common fungal disease affecting rice sheath and leaves

Overview

Sheath blight is caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani. It spreads rapidly in warm and humid conditions and reduces rice yield.

Symptoms

  • Oval greenish-gray lesions on leaf sheaths
  • White or gray fungal growth
  • Leaves dry and collapse in severe cases

Causes

  • High humidity
  • Dense planting
  • Excess nitrogen fertilizer

Treatment

  • Apply recommended fungicides
  • Improve field drainage

Prevention

  • Use resistant rice varieties
  • Maintain proper plant spacing
